AI Estimator Quick Reference Guide
# AI Estimator Quick Reference ## 📐 Ice & Water Shield Calculation Rules ### Eaves Coverage: - **Overhang ≤ 12 inches:** Apply 3 feet of ice & water from edge - **Overhang > 12 inches:** Apply 6 feet of ice & water from edge - **Default assumption (satellite):** 6 feet (safer for insurance) ### Valley Coverage: - Apply ice & water shield for **full length** of all valleys - Typically 36" wide rolls ### Calculation Formula: - Ice & Water (eaves) = Eave LF × (Coverage Width ÷ 3) - Example: 100 LF eave × (6 ft ÷ 3) = 200 LF ice & water - Ice & Water (valleys) = Valley LF - Total Ice & Water = Eaves + Valleys ## 💬 Common Commands ### Material Adjustments - **"add 10% waste"** - Increases material quantities by 10% - **"add 15% waste"** - Increases material quantities by 15% ### Surcharges - **"apply steep roof"** - Adds 20% steep roof surcharge - **"apply high roof"** - Adds 15% high roof surcharge (2-3 stories) - **"add O&P"** or **"add overhead and profit"** - Adds 15% O&P ### Adding Items - **"add 5 LF of valley flashing"** - Adds specific item with quantity - **"add ice and water shield"** - Adds item from price list ### Pricing Changes - Just switch the dropdown - AI will recalculate everything ## 🛰️ Satellite Mode Tips ### Best Practices ✅ Enter complete address with city and state ✅ Wait 30-60 seconds for analysis ✅ Check confidence scores - above 85% is good ⚠️ Below 85% confidence? Upload actual report instead ### Common Issues - **No measurements showing?** Check browser console (F12) for errors - **Low confidence?** Try document upload mode instead - **Wrong address?** Click "New Property" to start over ## 📄 Document Upload Tips ### Supported Files ✅ GAF QuickMeasure PDFs ✅ Hover reports ✅ EagleView reports ✅ Xactimate ESX files ✅ Any PDF with measurements ### Upload Process 1. Click paperclip icon 📎 2. Select file 3. Click "Analyze" 4. Wait 10-20 seconds 5. Review line items ## 🎯 Pro Tips ### For Roofing Jobs - Always add 10% waste for shingles - Add steep surcharge for 8/12 pitch or higher - Add high roof surcharge for 2+ stories - Include O&P for insurance jobs - Ice & water: 6 ft at eaves (default) + full valleys ### Common Pricing Sources - **Xactimate New**: Most insurance companies - **Symbility**: Some carriers prefer this - **Custom**: For cash/retail jobs ### Material List - Click "Generate Material List" after creating estimate - Shows exactly what to purchase - Includes bundles/boxes needed - Separates materials from labor ## 🚀 Workflow Example ### Complete Estimate in 2 Minutes: 1. Enter address in Satellite Mode 2. Wait for AI analysis (30s) 3. Type "add 10% waste" 4. Type "apply steep roof" (if needed) 5. Type "add O&P" 6. Fill customer info 7. Click "Save to CRM" ### Or Upload Report: 1. Click Document Mode 2. Upload PDF 3. Click Analyze 4. Make adjustments 5. Save! ## 💰 Pricing Quick Reference ### Typical Roofing Prices (Xactimate) - Shingles (Architectural): ~$350/SQ - Ridge Cap: ~$8-10/LF - Valley Metal: ~$12/LF - Drip Edge: ~$6/LF - Ice & Water Shield: ~$2-5/LF - Underlayment: ~$30-45/SQ - Tear-off: ~$60-75/SQ ### Common Surcharges - Steep Roof (8/12+): 20% - High Roof (2 stories): 15% - O&P: 10-15% - Waste: 10-15% ## ⚙️ Settings to Configure ### Before First Use: 1. Go to Settings → Configure AI Estimator 2. Set your specialty (Roofing, Siding, etc.) 3. Set default pricing source 4. Add primary materials you use ### Pricing Sources: - Go to Items → Connect integrations - Import Xactimate, Symbility, or custom prices - Mark favorites for quick access ### NEVER Auto-Select: - ❌ Wood shake materials (unless specifically requested) - ❌ Ridge vents (use ridge CAP instead) - ❌ Aluminum starter (use asphalt starter) - ✅ ONLY asphalt shingle materials by default

Training the AI Estimator
Training the AI Estimator: A Comprehensive Guide
The AI Estimator is a powerful tool that learns from your specific business data. To get the best results, you need to "teach" it your pricing, preferred materials, and line item ordering. Here is a detailed guide on how to do that effectively.

1. Uploading Past Estimates (The Foundation)
The most effective way to train the AI is by showing it examples of "perfect" estimates you've created in the past.

Format: PDF files are best. You can also use detailed CSV exports if available.
Quantity: Aim for 5-10 approved estimates that cover different types of jobs (e.g., "Full Roof Replacement", "Gutter Repair", "Siding Installation").
What the AI Learns:
Line Item Order: It notices that you always list "Starter Shingles" before "Field Shingles".
Pricing: It learns your base labor rates and material markups.
Language: It adopts your specific naming conventions (e.g., using "Arch Shingle" instead of "Architectural Shingles").
How to Upload: Go to Knowledge Base, click "Add Article", and use the file upload section. Title the article "Training Estimate - [Job Type]".
2. "Instructions-Only" Articles (The Rules)
Sometimes, examples aren't enough. You need to give the AI explicit rules. Create new Knowledge Base articles with the category "Processes" or "Pricing" and write clear instructions.

Examples of Good Instructions:
Waste Factors: "For all asphalt shingle roofs, calculate 10% waste for gable roofs and 15% for hip roofs."
Upgrades: "Always offer a 'Good, Better, Best' option for full replacements. 'Good' is 3-tab, 'Better' is Architectural, 'Best' is Impact Resistant."
Fees: "Always include a $450 dumpster fee line item for any job over 20 squares."
Minimums: "Our minimum repair charge is $350. If the line items total less than this, add a 'Minimum Service Charge' line item to make up the difference."
3. The Feedback Loop (Continuous Improvement)
Training isn't a one-time event. The AI learns every time you interact with it.

Edit Estimates: When the AI generates an estimate, don't just accept it. If it missed a line item or got a price wrong, fix it in the editor. The AI observes these corrections.
Rate Output: If the AI does a great job, give it a "thumbs up" or positive rating. If it messes up, give it a "thumbs down" and explain why in the comments (e.g., "You forgot the ridge vent").
4. Advanced Tips
Regional Pricing: If you work in multiple states, create articles specifying "Ohio Pricing" vs "Florida Pricing".
Seasonal Rules: "From November to March, add a 'Winter Safety Surcharge' of 5%."

Setting Up Workflows
Setting Up Workflows: Automation Masterclass
Workflows allow you to build "If This, Then That" logic to automate your business processes. Stop doing manual follow-ups and let the system handle it.

1. The Basics
Triggers: The event that starts the automation (e.g., "New Lead Created", "Status Changed to 'Proposal Sent'", "Tag Added").
Conditions: Filters to ensure the workflow only runs for specific cases (e.g., "Only if Lead Source is 'Facebook Ads'", "Only if Value is > $10,000").
Actions: What the system actually does (e.g., "Send Email", "Create Task", "Update Field", "Send SMS").
2. Step-by-Step: Creating a "New Lead Welcome" Workflow
Go to Communication > Workflow Automation and click "New Workflow".
Trigger: Select "Entity Created" -> "Lead".
Action 1 (Email): Add a "Send Email" action.
Template: Choose "Welcome Email" (or write a new one).
Subject: "Thanks for contacting [Company Name]!"
Body: "Hi {{lead.first_name}}, thanks for reaching out..."
Action 2 (Internal Notification): Add a "Send Notification" action.
Target: "Sales Manager"
Message: "New lead assigned: {{lead.name}}"
Action 3 (Task): Add a "Create Task" action.
Title: "Call new lead {{lead.name}}"
Due: "T+1 hours" (Due in 1 hour)
Assignee: "Lead Owner"
3. Popular Workflow Recipes
Estimate Follow-up: Trigger: "Estimate Status = Sent". Wait 2 Days. Check Condition: "Status is still Sent". Action: Send "Just checking in" email.
Review Request: Trigger: "Job Status = Completed". Wait 1 Hour. Action: Send SMS with Google Review link.
Stalled Lead Re-engagement: Trigger: "Lead Status = Contacted". Wait 14 Days. Check Condition: "No Activity". Action: Send "Are you still interested?" email.
4. Testing
Always test your workflows! Use the "Test Workflow" button to simulate a run with a dummy lead to ensure emails look right and tasks are created correctly.

Using the Accounting Dashboard: Financial Mastery
Accounting Dashboard: Financial Mastery
The Accounting Dashboard is your financial command center. Here is how to use every feature to keep your books balanced.

1. Understanding the Metrics
Total Revenue: The sum of all Payments with status 'Received'. Does not include pending invoices.
Net Profit: (Total Revenue) - (Expenses + Payouts + Commissions). This is your real take-home.
Accounts Receivable (A/R): Money owed to you. Sum of all 'Sent' or 'Overdue' invoices.
Accounts Payable (A/P): Money you owe. Sum of pending Payouts and Bills.
2. Recording Expenses
Don't let small costs eat your profit. Record everything.

Receipt Scanner: On mobile, click "Scan Receipt" to auto-extract vendor and amount.
Recurring Expenses: For software or rent, check "Recurring" to have it auto-create each month.
Job Costing: Select a "Customer" or "Project" when adding an expense to calculate true job profitability.
3. The Journal Entry Tool
Use this for advanced accounting adjustments.

Depreciation: Debit "Depreciation Expense", Credit "Assets".
Owner's Equity: If you put personal money into the business, Credit "Owner's Equity", Debit "Cash".
Opening Balances: When migrating from QuickBooks, use a Journal Entry to set your starting account balances.

Calendar Integration Guide: Sync & Scheduling
A properly synced calendar is crucial for avoiding double-booking and ensuring your team knows where to be.

1. Connecting Google Calendar
We support a robust 2-way sync with Google Calendar.

Navigate: Go to Settings > Integrations > Google Calendar.
Authorization: Click "Connect" and allow the requested permissions. You must authorize access to your calendar events.
Calendar Selection:
Primary Calendar: Choose which Google Calendar you want to sync to (e.g., your main work calendar). Events created in the CRM will appear here.
Check for Conflicts: Select multiple calendars (e.g., "Work", "Personal", "Family") that the system should check. If you have a "Dentist Appointment" on your Personal calendar, the CRM will block that time off so you can't be booked.
2. How Sync Works
CRM to Google (Outbound): When you create an Inspection or Meeting in the CRM, it appears instantly on your Google Calendar.
Google to CRM (Inbound): Events created in Google (e.g., "Lunch with Mom") are pulled into the CRM as "Busy" blocks. The details are kept private (shown as "Busy") unless you change the visibility settings.
Updates: Changing a time in Google updates the CRM, and vice-versa.
3. Appointment Booking Logic
When you or a customer (via the booking link) tries to schedule a time:

The system checks your CRM Calendar for existing jobs.
It checks your Synced Google Calendars for external conflicts.
It checks your Working Hours (set in Staff Profile).
It only offers times that are truly free across all these sources.
4. Troubleshooting
"Events not showing up": Sync runs every 5-10 minutes. For an instant refresh, go to the Integrations page and click "Sync Now".
"Double Booking": Ensure you have selected ALL relevant calendars in the "Check for Conflicts" section of the integration settings.

Training the AI Estimator
Training the AI Estimator: A Comprehensive Guide
The AI Estimator is a powerful tool that learns from your specific business data. To get the best results, you need to "teach" it your pricing, preferred materials, and line item ordering. Here is a detailed guide on how to do that effectively.

1. Uploading Past Estimates (The Foundation)
The most effective way to train the AI is by showing it examples of "perfect" estimates you've created in the past.

Format: PDF files are best. You can also use detailed CSV exports if available.
Quantity: Aim for 5-10 approved estimates that cover different types of jobs (e.g., "Full Roof Replacement", "Gutter Repair", "Siding Installation").
What the AI Learns:
Line Item Order: It notices that you always list "Starter Shingles" before "Field Shingles".
Pricing: It learns your base labor rates and material markups.
Language: It adopts your specific naming conventions (e.g., using "Arch Shingle" instead of "Architectural Shingles").
How to Upload: Go to Knowledge Base, click "Add Article", and use the file upload section. Title the article "Training Estimate - [Job Type]".
2. "Instructions-Only" Articles (The Rules)
Sometimes, examples aren't enough. You need to give the AI explicit rules. Create new Knowledge Base articles with the category "Processes" or "Pricing" and write clear instructions.

Examples of Good Instructions:
Waste Factors: "For all asphalt shingle roofs, calculate 10% waste for gable roofs and 15% for hip roofs."
Upgrades: "Always offer a 'Good, Better, Best' option for full replacements. 'Good' is 3-tab, 'Better' is Architectural, 'Best' is Impact Resistant."
Fees: "Always include a $450 dumpster fee line item for any job over 20 squares."
Minimums: "Our minimum repair charge is $350. If the line items total less than this, add a 'Minimum Service Charge' line item to make up the difference."
3. The Feedback Loop (Continuous Improvement)
Training isn't a one-time event. The AI learns every time you interact with it.

Edit Estimates: When the AI generates an estimate, don't just accept it. If it missed a line item or got a price wrong, fix it in the editor. The AI observes these corrections.
Rate Output: If the AI does a great job, give it a "thumbs up" or positive rating. If it messes up, give it a "thumbs down" and explain why in the comments (e.g., "You forgot the ridge vent").
4. Advanced Tips
Regional Pricing: If you work in multiple states, create articles specifying "Ohio Pricing" vs "Florida Pricing".
Seasonal Rules: "From November to March, add a 'Winter Safety Surcharge' of 5%."

Setting Up Workflows
Setting Up Workflows: Automation Masterclass
Workflows allow you to build "If This, Then That" logic to automate your business processes. Stop doing manual follow-ups and let the system handle it.

1. The Basics
Triggers: The event that starts the automation (e.g., "New Lead Created", "Status Changed to 'Proposal Sent'", "Tag Added").
Conditions: Filters to ensure the workflow only runs for specific cases (e.g., "Only if Lead Source is 'Facebook Ads'", "Only if Value is > $10,000").
Actions: What the system actually does (e.g., "Send Email", "Create Task", "Update Field", "Send SMS").
2. Step-by-Step: Creating a "New Lead Welcome" Workflow
Go to Communication > Workflow Automation and click "New Workflow".
Trigger: Select "Entity Created" -> "Lead".
Action 1 (Email): Add a "Send Email" action.
Template: Choose "Welcome Email" (or write a new one).
Subject: "Thanks for contacting [Company Name]!"
Body: "Hi {{lead.first_name}}, thanks for reaching out..."
Action 2 (Internal Notification): Add a "Send Notification" action.
Target: "Sales Manager"
Message: "New lead assigned: {{lead.name}}"
Action 3 (Task): Add a "Create Task" action.
Title: "Call new lead {{lead.name}}"
Due: "T+1 hours" (Due in 1 hour)
Assignee: "Lead Owner"
3. Popular Workflow Recipes
Estimate Follow-up: Trigger: "Estimate Status = Sent". Wait 2 Days. Check Condition: "Status is still Sent". Action: Send "Just checking in" email.
Review Request: Trigger: "Job Status = Completed". Wait 1 Hour. Action: Send SMS with Google Review link.
Stalled Lead Re-engagement: Trigger: "Lead Status = Contacted". Wait 14 Days. Check Condition: "No Activity". Action: Send "Are you still interested?" email.
4. Testing
Always test your workflows! Use the "Test Workflow" button to simulate a run with a dummy lead to ensure emails look right and tasks are created correctly.

Accounting Dashboard: Financial Mastery
The Accounting Dashboard is your financial command center. Here is how to use every feature to keep your books balanced.

1. Understanding the Metrics
Total Revenue: The sum of all Payments with status 'Received'. Does not include pending invoices.
Net Profit: (Total Revenue) - (Expenses + Payouts + Commissions). This is your real take-home.
Accounts Receivable (A/R): Money owed to you. Sum of all 'Sent' or 'Overdue' invoices.

Use this for advanced accounting adjustments.

Depreciation: Debit "Depreciation Expense", Credit "Assets".
Owner's Equity: If you put personal money into the business, Credit "Owner's Equity", Debit "Cash".

# AI Estimator Quick Reference ## 📐 Ice & Water Shield Calculation Rules ### Eaves Coverage: - **Overhang ≤ 12 inches:** Apply 3 feet of ice & water from edge - **Overhang > 12 inches:** Apply 6 feet of ice & water from edge - **Default assumption (satellite):** 6 feet (safer for insurance) ### Valley Coverage: - Apply ice & water shield for **full length** of all valleys - Typically 36" wide rolls ### Calculation Formula: - Ice & Water (eaves) = Eave LF × (Coverage Width ÷ 3) - Example: 100 LF eave × (6 ft ÷ 3) = 200 LF ice & water - Ice & Water (valleys) = Valley LF - Total Ice & Water = Eaves + Valleys ## 💬 Common Commands ### Material Adjustments - **"add 10% waste"** - Increases material quantities by 10% - **"add 15% waste"** - Increases material quantities by 15% ### Surcharges - **"apply steep roof"** - Adds 20% steep roof surcharge - **"apply high roof"** - Adds 15% high roof surcharge (2-3 stories) - **"add O&P"** or **"add overhead and profit"** - Adds 15% O&P ### Adding Items - **"add 5 LF of valley flashing"** - Adds specific item with quantity - **"add ice and water shield"** - Adds item from price list ### Pricing Changes - Just switch the dropdown - AI will recalculate everything ## 🛰️ Satellite Mode Tips ### Best Practices ✅ Enter complete address with city and state ✅ Wait 30-60 seconds for analysis ✅ Check confidence scores - above 85% is good ⚠️ Below 85% confidence? Upload actual report instead ### Common Issues - **No measurements showing?** Check browser console (F12) for errors - **Low confidence?** Try document upload mode instead - **Wrong address?** Click "New Property" to start over ## 📄 Document Upload Tips ### Supported Files ✅ GAF QuickMeasure PDFs ✅ Hover reports ✅ EagleView reports ✅ Xactimate ESX files ✅ Any PDF with measurements ### Upload Process 1.
